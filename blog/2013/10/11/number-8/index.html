End of Week 4

This week has been difficult. If I’m able to figure out some of the parts to each program, I still can’t piece them all together to see how the whole thing works. So far Ruby has been easier to work with but that could be because I’ve had some more time to spend working with it than with Rails. It seems like in the long run I might prefer Rails. I have been trying to pick apart a Ruby tutorial game, where a ship is moved across the screen to collect items. There are plenty of variables to affect the speed of the ship and generation of stars and I’ve been testing them all out, adding in notes to help me remember. I found that Codeschool will work on Chrome, as Safari wasn’t loading theings correctly. Somestimes I would try to do some Zombies on my Mac but the videos wouldn’t load. Still working on Ruby Bits but it’s been slow because of display problems on Safari. THe slides are zoomed-in too much that it’s not easy to switch between them while answering the questions. I want to show my younger cousins these courses over the holidays to give them an idea of what coding is. The Dev Stackup on Tuesday evening was interesting. I liked hearing about the things everyone did at work and what these creations were used for. I start to wonder how many things I use are coded by people nearby. I also liked hearing that places are hiring, which has never been the case since I finished radiology school. That’s more of an incentive for me to study and work hard.
